Notes on Open Source Permaculture

Vision Statements

  • Clear outline and structure that itself teaches
  • Teaches the connections between "elements"
  • Clear interconnections between "elements"
  • Easily editable, narratively independent "elements"
  • Free of "the voice of authors"
  • Great user experience
  • Pushes the "turn knowledge into action" boundary
  • Ability to take offline
  • Ability to perfect knowledge and process
  • Be the work of the community (community generative)
  • Ability to consolidate all current information from different types of sources
    • design courses (open and closed)
    • books
    • forums
    • blogs
    • personal experience of practitioners
  • High degree of "open intelligence" or a high value of information that is open and accessible

Breakdowns With Current System

  • Low accessibility of vast amounts of permaculture information
  • Low degree of growth of information (lots of words, little new info)
  • Low ability to generate community
  • Current initiatives drivien moreby profit than by sharing in the mission of spreading the lifestyle of permaculture
  • Superflouous narratives, social talk, filler info in existing literature
  • People feeling as if they need "approval" from the existing "experts" to be successful

Learn How To's

  • Activity of setting up and maintaining permaculture system

Desires

  • Intelligent uptake and structuring of best available information
    • ... to make this simple
    • ... to make this streamlined
    • ... to make this geared towards activity
  • Designed to produce real world food
  • Easy to act now, learn the details over the time

GOALS

  • Good/great results for all people (not perfect results for experts)
  • You dont have to go through years of learning for good-to-great results.
  • For the people that "...STILL NEED FOOD"
  • Simplify everything, get the design obsession out of permaculture
  • Make permaculture human again
