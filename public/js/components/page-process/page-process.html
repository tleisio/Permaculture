Process

This page outlines the permaculture process, taken as an activity guide and to-do list if people want to start down the path of practicing permaculture. A large amount of "process" found today has tons of what Tim calls "academic content" that inhibits a "pick it up and go" mentality. The process below is attempting to resolve that challenge.

Initial & Ongoing Learning

  1. Learn about your context.
    1. Global & Regional
      • weather patterns - exercise: gather and document
      • geological history
      • major rivers and tributaries - exercise: find and upload national/regional watershed images
      • topography
    2. Local/State
      • watersheds - exercise: find and upload state/county watershed images
      • topography
      • plant life pre-civilization / native plants / old growth
      • mantle types
      • soil types
    3. Site (all exercises...?)
      • sectors
      • map topography - exercise: map your site contours
      • take inventory of existing plants - exercise: document existing plants
      • observe and map water flow
      • soil type and infiltration
      • identify and microclimate
      • map access (roads, paths, and freq of use)
      • zones
    4. If you didn't yet do 2, do that.
  2. Learn about plants and their biology.
